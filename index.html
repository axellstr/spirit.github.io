About Us

Welcome to Our Creative World

Welcome to Spirit Lab, a sanctuary dedicated to the ancient art of shamanic healing
through the power of plants. At Spirit Lab, we honor the deep connection between
nature and the human spirit, offering transformative experiences guided by age-old
shamanic traditions.

Our center is a haven for those seeking to explore the healing virtues of plants and
engage in personal spiritual growth. We believe in the synergistic relationship
between the earth's natural remedies and the human soul, and our expert shamans
are here to guide you through this journey.

Each session at Spirit Lab is a step towards inner harmony, empowered by the wisdom
of plant medicine and the nurturing guidance of our experienced practitioners. Join us
in this sacred space to embark on a journey of healing, self-discovery, and reconnection
with the natural world.

Our Master Plants

Bobinsana

Calliandra angustifolia

Calliandra angustifolia, widely known as bobinsana, is a small tree indigenous to the Amazon Basin, thriving in riparian zones. Renowned for its therapeutic properties, it is traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and pain-relieving effects, and is believed to boost the immune system and improve emotional well-being.

Noya Rao

Tree of Light

Noya Rao, a rare and mystical tree native to the Amazon rainforest, is traditionally revered for its spiritual and healing properties. Often referred to as the "Tree of Light" or "Camino a la Verdad", it is known for its luminescent bark and leaves, believed to illuminate the path to truth and enlightenment. This tree is commonly used in shamanic practices for its potent spiritual significance and is said to enhance dream work, spiritual insights, and emotional healing.

Jurema

Mimosa tenuiflora

Jurema, scientifically known as Mimosa tenuiflora or Mimosa hostilis, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, known for its rich cultural and medicinal significance. It's revered for its potent bark, used traditionally in healing ceremonies and for its psychoactive properties. Jurema is often employed in natural medicine for skin healing, due to its antiseptic, analgesic, and regenerative qualities, and plays a vital role in indigenous spiritual rituals, where it's used to induce visionary states and spiritual introspection.
